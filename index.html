Cars are motor vehicles designed primarily for transporting people. They typically have four wheels, an internal combustion engine or electric motor, and seat one to eight passengers. Cars revolutionized transportation by offering personal mobility and flexibility.

This are some random stats about cars

The first car accident happened in 1891 — and involved only one car! The driver hit a tree root and crashed into a hitching post. No other drivers were involved... because there were hardly any other cars!

In Switzerland, it’s illegal to slam your car door — at night! The law exists to prevent disturbing the peace. So if you’re angry... slam something quieter.

There’s a car smaller than a fridge — The Peel P50 is the tiniest production car ever made. It's so small, you can literally drag it like luggage.

Volkswagen once made a car that farted — well, kind of. Their ad campaign for the Golf TDI said it had “clean diesel,” but later they were caught cheating emissions tests. The cars “farted” out way more pollution than they claimed!

The modern automobile, as a practical and marketable vehicle for everyday use, is generally considered to have originated in 1886 with the German inventor Carl Benz's "Benz Patent-Motorwagen". However, the development of automobiles involved contributions from many inventors over time, with early prototypes using steam, electricity, and internal combustion engines.
